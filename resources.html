Sophomore Class President Platform


          My name is Sungmin Lee, and I am running for Sophomore Class President. As Sophomore Class President, I will increase inclusivity in events, take notice of smaller groups, recognize people for their contributions, understand the needs and wants of the student body, and provide opportunities for and know the importance of teen decisions; in other words, the I.N.P.U.T. principles.

Involve

          To increase the inclusivity of school events, I will ensure more opportunities in which students can be involved. Being the freshmen classes' president has taught me many valuable lessons, especially in managing and running events/fundraisers. Also, by being a part of many clubs, like robotics and CSF, I know how powerful it is to feel involved. As Class President, I seek to create a sense of belonging among students so they can feel like they are part of the sophomore class, not just superficially. Additionally, I will have more class events that are connected to the class itself, such as a talent show, and easy to participate events, like a raffle that just only requires a person to wear their class shirt to enter.

Notice

          In Branham, many activities occur, such as sports and clubs, so it can be difficult for students to know when the next event is. I will make sure that people can receive notice of events through emails, social media, Remind, and websites so they can stay connected to the class. Furthermore, I will spread awareness for lesser-known clubs and provide opportunities for them to work with the ASB Sophomore Class, especially for clubs run by and for Sophomores. Additionally, I plan on making a Sophomore newsletter where students can be informed on nearing events, important links, and featuring a club of the month; which would have information that the club wants to advertise for 1 month. 

People Recognition 

          In our school, there are some incredible people that deserve recognition. This is why, I will be implementing the “Gifts for Kindness” program, where students will be able to share stories of people that they believe to deserve recognition in the newsletter. Bi-monthly 3 -5 people will be selected to receive gifts (personal goodie bags, bruin gear, etc.).

     Understanding Needs

          As students of Branham, it is important to be accurately represented, to have what you want accounted for. This is why one of my main priorities is to understand what the school needs and fulfill them. I will guarantee to provide accessibility for and answer questions and concerns from students. An idea I have is to create Q&A and update videos on Instagram to answer any questions and inform students about upcoming events, clubs, and general news.
  
Teen Decisions Matter

          As teens, it is important to express opinions and make decisions, because your voice matters. In this upcoming year, I will ensure students can give input and create changes through the classes' Instagram and newsletter.
          Voting for the right person is your responsibility, so vote for me. The one who will include others, bring notice, recognize people, understand the student body, and knows that teen decisions matter. This is why you should vote for me, Sungmin Lee, the best choice for the next Sophomore Class President.
